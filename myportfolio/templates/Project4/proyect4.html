QR code detector using Machine Learning

This project started as a tecnical challenge by well-regarded digital solutions company. At first, it was a bit overwhelming, and I didn't know where to begin. So, I decided to look up information online to get a better idea of how to tackle this challenge. I searched the internet to find articles, papers, and tutorials related to QR code detection. As I learned more, I realized that I needed to create a step-by-step plan to solve the QR code detection puzzle. This plan would involve using algorithms and models to process images and locate QR codes.

Step 1: Data Collection:

The project commenced with the intriguing task of gathering QR code images from the vast expanse of the internet. Additionally, I reached out to friends, urging them to contribute any QR images they might have. This collaborative data collection process laid the foundation for a diverse and comprehensive dataset.

The initial database contained just 50 images, each hosting QR codes in different orientations—a modest yet promising beginning.

You can find here the first batch of images.

These images served as the project's foundation, showcasing QR codes in various contexts. With this starting point, I was ready to move on to preprocessing, feature extraction, and model selection, each step building the ultimate objective of a robust QR extractor application.

Step 2: Preprocessing

My initial approach was to harness the capabilities of the OpenCV library, a versatile tool renowned in the area of computer vision. The initial steps involved applying Gaussian blur to refine image quality by mitigating noise and imperfections. Subsequently, grayscale conversion simplified visual complexity, enhancing QR code prominence against diverse backgrounds.

Aiming for consistency, images underwent rescaling to a standardized dimension, aligning disparate sizes for uniformity. Further optimization took form through pixel binarization, where pixels were transformed into binary values using a threshold, accentuating QR code edges. As these preprocessing layers converged, I approached feature extraction with confidence of my efforts.

However, in the subsequent phase, a remarkable discovery reshaped my trajectory. An online tool that streamlined preprocessing effortlessly. This tool seamlessly integrated into the process, rendering my OpenCV endeavors as just a valuable learning experience.

Step 3: Feature Extraction

Upon entering the task of feature extraction, a clear step emerged: the need to capture the x and y coordinates of each corner for each QR code across the 50 images. This task appeared time-intensive and potentially deadline-threatening. In search of solutions, the internet led me to Roboflow—an online tool that seamlessly streamlined the process. This discovery rendered my earlier OpenCV efforts redundant, as Roboflow effortlessly handled image preprocessing and automated dataset separation into training, validation, and testing.

As I uploaded the images in roboflow’s dashboard, the process of labeling and annotating them became a fluid process. With a drag of the mouse, rectangles could be drawn around the QR codes, and the corresponding coordinates were automatically captured and compiled into an exportable text file. This facilitated the extraction of vital data, necessary for the training of future models.
