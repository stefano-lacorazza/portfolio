When I set out on the daunting task of finding a new apartment to rent in Colombia, I found myself confronted with the challenge of navigating the ever-changing and often unpredictable housing rental market. The quest to find a suitable place that aligns with my budget and preferences led me to realize that predicting house rent prices is indeed a real-world problem faced by countless individuals. It became evident that an innovative solution was needed to aid not only myself but also others in their pursuit of the perfect rental accommodation.

Inspired by this personal struggle and the desire to help fellow renters, I embarked on a mission to develop a machine learning project aimed at predicting house rent prices in Colombia. By leveraging the power of data science techniques and the vast troves of rental market data available, this project seeks to bring a new level of efficiency and accuracy to the process of estimating rental costs.

Step 1: Gathering data

With the vision set, the first step in this project was to gather the data necessary to train the predictive model. To achieve this, I turned to one of Colombia's main rental websites, Metro Cruadrado, a platform known for its vast collection of property listings across the country. Employing web scraping techniques, I embarked on extracting relevant rental information from the website, including property features, location, and corresponding rental prices. By creating a systematic database from this trove of data, I aimed to build a robust foundation for training the machine learning model. The web scraping process involved handling complex data structures and ensuring data integrity. As I successfully compiled this extensive dataset, the stage was set for the subsequent phases of the project, where data exploration, preprocessing, and feature engineering would come into play to lay the groundwork for a powerful and accurate rent price prediction model.

Step 2: Cleaning the data

With the dataset in hand, the next phase of the project involved data cleaning and feature identification. To ensure the reliability and accuracy of our model, it was imperative to address missing values, outliers, and inconsistencies within the data. During this process, I focused on identifying key features that significantly influence rent prices. City and location data were paramount, as they played a vital role in capturing regional variations and urban dynamics.

Furthermore, attributes like the number of rooms, bathrooms, and property area emerged as essential indicators of rental prices, offering valuable insights into the housing supply and demand dynamics. Additionally, I considered the antiquity of properties as a crucial feature, as older buildings might have distinct rental pricing patterns compared to newer developments.

Through data exploration and feature engineering, I curated a dataset with informative attributes that were ready for the subsequent stages of model training and evaluation. Armed with this comprehensive and cleaned data, I was eager to delve into the realm of machine learning algorithms to create a powerful rent price prediction model.

Step 3: Training the model

With the refined dataset in hand, I proceeded to the phase of training the prediction model. To capture the complexity of the rental market, I explored a diverse set of machine learning algorithms, including linear regression, random forest regression, and Support Vector Machine (SVM).

Starting with linear regression, it provided a baseline understanding of the data's linear relationships but showed limitations in capturing the non-linear complexities prevalent in the rental market. SVM exhibited better performance by finding optimal hyperplanes in high-dimensional spaces, but it was not as effective.

The breakthrough came with the random forest regression, which demonstrated remarkable adaptability to the data's non-linear patterns and robustness against overfitting. By aggregating the predictions of multiple decision trees, this ensemble model outperformed the others in accuracy. Evaluating the models based on the mean absolute percentage error (MAPE) as the metric, it became evident that the random forest regression with 100 estimators consistently yielded the most promising results. Its ability to capture intricate relationships between features and rental prices, along with its resilience to outliers, made it the ideal choice for our rent price prediction model. I was specially satisfied when it accurately predicted my current apartment’s rent cost with an error of less than 5%.

Step 4: Analize the results

With the model at hand, the final step of the project involved a comprehensive analysis of the results. This analysis aimed to gain deeper insights into the factors driving rent prices in Colombia and to identify the most influential features affecting rental rates.

Upon close examination of the model's output, it became evident that the area of the property emerged as the most critical determinant of rent prices. Larger properties tended to command higher rents, which wasn’t all that surprising. Following closely in significance were the location and antiquity of the property. The model highlighted the strong impact of location on rental prices, with properties situated in prime neighborhoods commanding premium rates, a characteristic well known in Colombia’s house market. Moreover, the antiquity of a property played a substantial role in shaping rent prices, with newer buildings often fetching higher rents compared to older counterparts.

This in-depth analysis not only validated the effectiveness of the random forest regression model but also shed light on the underlying dynamics of Colombia's rental market. By understanding the most crucial factors driving rent prices, this model could serve as a powerful tool for both renters and landlords to make informed decisions in their rental transactions.

As this project came to a close, I felt a sense of pride and accomplishment in developing a machine learning solution that could potentially help the way people search for rental properties in Colombia. The combination of accurate predictions and valuable insights into key features had the potential to empower individuals in their apartment hunting endeavors.
